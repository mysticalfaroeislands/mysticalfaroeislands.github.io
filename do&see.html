From epic hikes to the edge of the world to eating locally sourced produce at a Michelin starred restaurant, spotting endangered puffins and kayaking the North Atlantic Ocean: these are the best things to do in the Faroe Islands.


BIKING

Long and empty roads. That's cyclist’s paradise!

The Faroe Islands are just that and more. The Faroe Islands give you time and space to enjoy the wild rugged scenery that these islands have to offer. On two wheels you feel the landscapes beneath you and you can spot wildlife that you may otherwise have missed. Enjoy a relaxed pace as you cycle across the archipelago exploring remote villages, distant islands and the buzzing Nordic city of Torshavn.

Looking for cycling routes and bike maps? Here you can find and create your best bike routes.


BIRDWATCHING

It’s like the Faroe Islands were dreamt up for migratory birds.

Each of the 18 isles is made of volcanic cliffs and heathland perched in fishy seas. This wind-whipped, wave-washed landscape is exactly why 300 species of birds descend on the Faroe Islands to breed and raise their sprogs each spring. With only 50,000 human residents and few predators, the birds pretty much have the run of the place.

Birdwatching in the Faroe Islands does not equire a lot of equipment. In some places, you can get close to birds like puffins, birdwatching can be done easily with the naked eye. However, most birds do not allow such close observations. That's why it is recommended to use binoculars.

Mykines is definitely the best part of the islands for birdwatching, hundreds of cute puffins nestled in burrows in the clifftops during the summer months.


DINNING

Faroese cuisine is composed primarily of lamb and fish which are prepared and served in a variety of ways. Other important foods are: skerpikjøt (type of dried mutton), other seafood, whale meat, blubber, garnatálg, Faroese puffins, and potatoes.

It is difficult to choose just few restaurants, but these 5 get definitely most attention: Koks, Barbara Fish House and Raest.


FISHING

The Faroe Islands are synonymous with fish. Faroe Islanders have fished for centuries, and fishing still plays an essential role in the community.

Whether by boat or from shore, there are plenty of opportunities to fish in the Faroe Islands. With the ocean never further than 5 km away – no matter where you’re located – it is effortless to grab a rod and go fishing whenever you want.

These types of fishes can be catched: Cod, Haddock, Flounder, Shark, Ling, Colefish, Halibut, Plaice, Catfish, Maceral, Monkfish, Whiting, Squid, Redfish, Herring, Pollock, among many others.

SURFING

People visit the Faroes for many different reasons, but few mention surfing as one of them. Not yet, that is.

There are great surfing opportunities in the Faroe Islands, but weather conditions on the islands means it is imperative that surfers become familiar with surfing guidelines before heading out.

Before you decide to try surfing, check out Faroe Islands Surf Guide.


SHOPPING

If you’re looking for souvenirs to carry back home, keep an eye out for traditional Faroese handicrafts. Handcrafted silver jewellery, knitwear and ceramics are among the most popular of local wares, and are available in shops and markets across the islands. If you’re at all interested in postal stamps, you might like to buy some while you’re in the Faroes- the islands print wonderfully colourful stamps, many of them featuring the local wildlife.


HORSE RIDING

Enjoy a unique experience riding on a Faroese pony, a breed that arrived with the settlers of the Faroe Islands more than 1000 years ago.

Definitely check Fjallaross. It is the only company in the Faroe Islands that lets you ride a Faroese Pony in the wild Faroese Nature. You will be riding without saddles and learn that the ponies can thrive without the use of horseshoes.


DIVING

he Faroe Islands offer some of the cleanest waters in the world and an amazing assortment of cold water marine life.

The relatively constant water temperatures and varied seabed of the Faroe Islands has encouraged a wide array of marine fauna from sea cucumbers, sea urchins, star fish, mussels, crabs, shrimps and brightly coloured nudibranchs. There is some spectacular scenery and topography both on land and beneath the water where you can explore rocky drop-offs, seaweed forests, deep gullies or gentle slopes.

Water temperatures range from 4-10°C, visibility is best during winter months but tends to be less during summer plankton blooms.

Events


The Islands may only have a population about 52,000, but when it comes to attracting big names like Mø, Toto or Lukas Graham, the Faroes punch well above theit weight.

G!Festival, taking place in mid-July, attracts both international headliners as well as Faroese artists. The G! name comes from its location, the village Gøta. Go for a swim in the ocean or take a dip in one of the popular wooden hot tubs on the sand, while listening to the artists. See more at www.gfestival.fo.

The another festival, The Summar Festival, held in August in Klaksvik, is the biggest music event in the Faroe Islands. The festival is well-suited for whole families with children. See more at www.summarfestivalur.com

Another popular festival is Voxbotn. It is a 12-hour pop festival held in Torshavn. See more at www.facebook.com/voxbotn.

Another festival held in Torshavn is Tórsfest. It is basically one huge street party with a dstage in Niles Finsens gøta. See more at www.visittorshavn.fo.

If you are looking for classical music, no need to be dissapointed. Summatonar Festival is held every summer at various locations across the islands. It means you can experience classical music concerts in churches or in village halls. See more at www.composers.fo.

Country&Blues Festival is another festival with local and internationl musicians. Held in the village of Sørvágur. See more at www.country.fo.

At any time of the year, there can be found "living room concerts". These concerts are held in someone's house, so you basically sit at somebody's living room listening to a concert. See more at www.heima.fo or www.hoymabit.fo.

It is worth to check The Nordic House that is host to various musical evenets. The house is located on the mountain side with a great view over Torshavn. It promotes the nordic and faroese culture.

If you want a full overview of all musical events and concerts, be sure to visitwww.whatson.fo/en.
